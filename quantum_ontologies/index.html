*enactment:

This webtext is designed to draw on readers' familiarity with traditional forms of scholarly texts, such as print-based articles. Yet, this webtext is decidely distinct from for-print scholarship as a matter of form, function, and execution, to name just a few ways. This is not a matter of stylistic pedantry—different enactments of texts are rhetorically consequential for how you experience this scholarship. Space and time are experienced iteratively and uniquely in each iteration of this scholarship—and therefore each works on the reader in unique ways. For example, linearity is possible here—but not priviledged; networked connectivity also reconfigures what is possible (and probable) in this webtext. The notification settings on your device also matter. Another spacetime configuration of this scholarship is available by selecting the PDF download option. The webtext is entagled with, but is not synonymous with, the PDF. Each enactment engages the scholarship—and technology, and the reader—in mututually informing but nevertheless distinct ways.

Put differently, the various enactments of this scholarship both perfrom as well as exist in a state of quantum superposition.
